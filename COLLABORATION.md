# Collaboration

Project IMACE (Integrated Modular Architecture for Cognitive Emulation) supports structured and open collaboration across individuals, research collectives, and organizations.

Collaboration within IMACE is designed to enable interdisciplinary research while maintaining clarity of scope, attribution, and governance.

---

## Principles of Collaboration

All collaboration within **Project  IMACE** is guided by the following principles:

- openness and transparency  
- interdisciplinary integration  
- reproducibility and verifiability  
- respect for attribution and origin  
- alignment with IMACE research scope  

Collaboration is treated as a structured research activity, not as informal or unregulated participation.

---

## Forms of Collaboration

Project IMACE supports multiple forms of collaboration, including:

- research design and conceptual development  
- code contributions and system implementation  
- documentation and knowledge development  
- experimental validation and replication  
- infrastructure and tooling support  
- review, critique, and advisory input  

Collaborations may occur at both project-level and cross-project levels.

For anything **Contact** : [commons@imace.online](mailto:commons@imace.online)

---

## THE ALTERN RESEARCH Cross-ARC Collaboration

As a cross-ARC research program within THE ALTERN Research ecosystem,Project IMACE enables coordinated collaboration across multiple research collectives, including:

- ArcBMM (Brains, Minds & Machines)  
- ArcAMI (Advanced Machine Intelligence)  
- ArcPsyCog (Psychology and Cognitive Science)  
- ArcCCS (Computational and Computer Science)  
- ArcDevSys (Development and Systems Research)  

These collaborations contribute domain-specific expertise and support the integrative research objectives of Project IMACE.

---

## Participation

Collaboration is open to:

- independent researchers  
- developers and contributors  
- academic participants  
- affiliated research groups  
- supporting organizations  

Participation is subject to alignment with Project IMACE's principles, scope, and contribution guidelines.

Participation does not grant governance authority.

---

## Contribution Alignment

All collaborative contributions must:

- align with the defined research scope  
- maintain clarity and documentation standards  
- preserve attribution and origin identity  
- comply with [licensing](LICENSING.md) and [intellectual property policies](IP_POLICY.md)  

Contributions that conflict with IMACE principles or structure may be reviewed, revised, or declined.

---

## Attribution and Recognition

All collaborators are expected to:

- acknowledge Project IMACE as the originating project where applicable.  
- maintain attribution in derivative or extended work.  
- avoid misrepresentation of unofficial work as official IMACE output.  

Recognition of contributions may be provided through documentation, authorship, or acknowledgment within project repositories.

---

## Governance and Coordination

All collaborations operate under the coordination of the Project IMACE governance [structure](STRUCTURE.md)

Major collaborative efforts, cross-project initiatives, and external engagements may require coordination approval.

---

## External Collaboration

IMACE may collaborate with external institutions, organizations, and independent initiatives.

Such collaborations:

- must remain consistent with Project IMACE’s research scope and principles
- must not compromise IMACE independence  
- must maintain transparency and attribution  

External collaboration does not imply transfer of ownership or governance authority.

---

## Summary

Collaboration within Project IMACE is open, structured, and interdisciplinary.

It is designed to enable collective research advancement while preserving clarity of responsibility, attribution, and governance integrity.
